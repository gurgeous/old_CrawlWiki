A woven silk bag. Opening it will release a number of spiders and other insects, as well as creating webs nearby. Evocations skill increases its effectiveness. There is a one in three chance of accidentally destroying the sack when using it.

A sack of spiders is an evokable item which, when used, generates a Spider's Nest-themed group of friendly monsters, and also generates multiple web traps on hostile monsters in your line of sight. Evocations skill affects both the strength and number of summoned allies.

The success rate depends on Evocations[1]: it is 30% at 0 skill, 60% at 10, 70% at 18, and 77% at 27.

The chance to destroy the sack on each use is 1/3 (regardless of Evocations skill).

Strategy

  • A sack of spiders is nearly useless without at least 8 or so levels of Evocations. If you haven't put in the training, don't expect any results from this beyond a couple of quickly destroyed meat shields.
  • Be careful where you move after using a sack of spiders. Stepping into one of your own generated web traps is a good way to earn yourself YASD.
  • Characters who train a lot of Evocations gain access to tarantellas and ghost moths. The former has a melee attack that can confuse any monster in the game, the latter has an irresistible anti-magic AoE attack.

History

  • Sacks of spiders were removed in 0.25.
  • Prior to 0.19, sacks of spiders didn't stack. Instead, each sack of spiders had 5-15 charges.
  • Prior to 0.18, there were giant mites, spiders and trapdoor spiders summoned if you evocation was still low.
  • In 0.15, the webs created when evoking a sack of spiders will target enemies, rather than being placed randomly.
  • Sacks of spiders were added in 0.13.
