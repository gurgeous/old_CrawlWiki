Armour acquirement

From CrawlWiki
Jump to: navigation, search
Version 0.13: This article may not be up to date for the latest stable release of Crawl.


Armour acquirement attempts to give a useful piece of armour. It will always be wearable. The exact algorithm is given below:

  • First, choose a slot to be acquired. This works differently for divine (i.e. Okawaru, or Xom) acquirements.
    • For divine acquirements, a flat 1/2 chance of body armour, otherwise it picks a random non-body slot.
    • For non-divine acquirements, iterate over wearable non-body slots, in the order (shield, cloak, helmet, gloves, boots). It has a 1/2 chance of selecting the first wearable slot, a 1/3 chance of selecting the next, and so on. If no non-body slot is selected, give body armour. Thus, non-divine acquirement is more likely to give non-body armour, which tends to be more useful.
      • If you are a naga or centaur, the armour has a flat chance of simply being converted to an appropriate barding at this point: 1/2 if you haven't seen a barding, or 1/4 if you have.
  • Next, ensure that the armour is wearable. Shield size is also handled at this point:
    • Spriggans, ogres, and trolls will have any body armour, gloves, boots, or bardings converted to robes. For divine acquirements, this means that the probability of receiving body armour is greater than 1/2, but for non-divine acquirements, the type of slot remains unchanged.
    • Spriggans will always have shields converted to bucklers.
    • With a (5+shield skill)/20 chance, ogres and trolls will have any shield upgraded to a large shield.
    • Draconians will have any body armour or barding converted to a helmet, gloves, or boots (equal chance). If a shield is selected for a draconian, with a (5+shield skill)/20 chance, it is upgraded to a large shield.
    • Nagas will see any centaur bardings or boots converted to naga bardings. If a shield is selected, upgrade to a large shield with a 5+(shield skill)/20 chance.
    • Centaurs will see any naga bardings or boots converted to centaur bardings. If a shield is selected, upgrade to a large shield with a (5+shield skill)/20 chance.
    • For all others, bardings are converted to boots at this point. Then, shield size is handled:
      • With a (15-shield skill)/20 chance, downgrade to a buckler.
      • Otherwise, with a (shield skill-10) chance, upgrade to a large shield.
    • Next, handle mutations. If boots or gloves are selected and unwearable due to claws/hooves/talons, give body armour instead.
    • If you have Horns 1-2 or Antennae 1-2, convert helmets to caps or wizard hats (equal chance). If you have 3 levels of either mutation, give body armour.
  • If a robe was selected by the above process (i.e. for spriggans, ogres, and trolls), apply the following:
    • With a 1/4 chance, it may be converted to an animal skin.
    • With a 1/20 chance, it may be converted to one of: troll leather armour (3/8 chance), steam dragon armour (3/8), swamp dragon armour (1/8), fire dragon armour (1/8).
    • For a non divine acquirement, there is a 1/5 chance of picking, with equal probabilities, a random dragon armour.
  • For other characters for whom body armour is selected, apply the following:
    • For divine acquirements:
      • Pick a random mundane body armour, with equal probabilities of each: robe, leather armour, ring mail, scale mail, chain mail, plate armour. Notably, divine acquirement does not consider armour skill here.
      • Convert to crystal plate armour with an armour_skill/150 chance.
      • Convert to an animal skin with a 1/12 chance.
    • For non-divine acquirements:
      • Select among the following: animal skin, robe, leather armour, ring mail, scale mail, chain mail, plate armour, crystal plate armour. This is weighted, with a bias against the heavier armours that is decreased by your skills. The exact algorithm here: as this list is iterated through, each armour is given a weight of (27-(armour_skill+3-3*i))^3, where i=0 for animal skins, 1 for robes, 2 for leather, etc. Iterate through the list. An armour is selected with a weight/total chance, where total is the sum of all weights up to this point (so at least an animal skin will always be selected).
      • The above formula is considered good only for spellcasters, so the following is also applied for melee fighters: if 1d(3*spellcasting + dodging) < 1d(2*armour_skill), then give a plate armour (75% chance) or crystal plate armour (25% chance).
    • Finally, for both non-divine and divine acquirements, there is a 1/20 chance of picking from the following list, with given individual probabilities: troll leather armour (20%), steam dragon armour (20%), mottled dragon armour (15%), swamp dragon armour (10%), fire dragon armour (10%), ice dragon armour (10%), storm dragon armour (5%), gold dragon armour (5%), pearl dragon armour (5%).

After all this, attempt to make a good, potentially artefact item of the given type.

A couple things to note here:

  • Fighters are in general far more likely to get worthwhile armour than spellcasters.
  • Okawaru does not consider your skills when gifting armour, except that he tends to give out CPA to higher skilled users. Hence the plate mails that he likes to gift to your MfSk.
  • Nowhere are skills considered when gifting dragon armours.
